The critical discovery: UpDown token order books return 404 (no book) for ALL tokens. There are no resting limit orders. Market makers respond to incoming orders only — they see your order and decide whether to fill it. This means:
• GTC limits just sit empty — nobody's on the other side
• FOK is actually better — it gets an instant answer (fill or no-fill)
• Liquidity is a function of the asset and the time — BTC is best, everything else is thin

The Bright Spot: Cheap Fills Actually Help

The BTC DOWN at 36¢ → $1 = +$12.08 trade is the poster child.

Final Assessment

The engine works. The edge is real. But the scale is smaller than paper suggests.

Paper shows a 100-trade, $3,851/day machine. Reality is a 10-15 trade, $30-80/day operation — still excellent ROI (70-120% daily on a $42 bankroll), but fundamentally limited by:
1. Liquidity — UpDown markets are thin, especially non-BTC
2. Fill rate — ~40-50% of signals become actual trades
3. Fill selection bias — MMs fill you when they think you're wrong

The v2 continuous evaluator question: In live, the main bottleneck is NOT timing (T-60s vs T-180s) — it's liquidity. Getting cheaper tokens via earlier entry is moot if the market maker isn't there to fill you. The bigger opportunity is maximising the trades that DO fill, which v5.6b's FOK approach addresses.

Next priorities:
1. Accumulate more live data (need 50+ resolved live trades for meaningful WR)
2. Focus on BTC — it's the only market with reliable fills
3. Optimise for time-of-day (08:00-18:00 UTC)
4. Build bankroll gradually ($42 → $100 → $500) with proven live WR

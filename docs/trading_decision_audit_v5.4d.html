What Actually Impacts Trading Decisions

The Novakash trading strategy follows a strict hierarchical decision tree. Each layer acts as a filter, and orders only proceed when all conditions pass. Understanding this flow is critical to diagnosing live performance.

1
VPIN Gate (≥0.45)

PRIMARY filter. No informed flow detected = NO TRADE. This is the gating mechanism for all signals.

2
Regime Detection

Based on VPIN value, apply different delta thresholds:

  • CASCADE (VPIN ≥ 0.65): momentum, delta ≥ 0.03% (scaled down at higher VPIN)
  • TRANSITION (0.55–0.65): momentum, delta ≥ 0.05%
  • NORMAL (0.45–0.55): momentum, delta ≥ 0.05%
3
Direction

ALL MOMENTUM (follow delta). Contrarian strategy was removed in v5.1.

4
Confidence Level

HIGH (δ > 0.10%), MODERATE (δ > 0.02%), LOW (δ > 0.005%). No trade below δ > 0.005%.

5
CoinGlass Modifier

Only OI delta > 0.10% is active (+0.10 boost). All other CG signals are zeroed.

6
CG Veto (NEW v5.4d)

Blocks trade if 3+ CoinGlass signals oppose direction. Veto fires even if modifier is zeroed.

7
Risk Checks

Exposure limit, daily loss limit, rate limiter, circuit breaker. Any breach = NO TRADE.

8
Pricing & Order

Gamma bestAsk + CLOB spread bump. Capped at 65¢ (5m) / 70¢ (15m), floor 30¢.

Key Insight: Filter Hierarchy

VPIN is the gating mechanism. Regime determines delta threshold. Direction, confidence, and modifiers apply only after VPIN passes. CG veto can kill otherwise-valid trades. Risk checks are last-mile guardrails. When a trade fails to fill or underperforms, trace back through this tree to identify which filter broke.

Root Cause Analysis

The CLOB spread reading 0.980 = 98¢ tells the story: there is essentially NO LIQUIDITY on the order book for UpDown tokens.

The Gamma vs. CLOB Gap

Gamma bestAsk is an indicative mid-market price—it reflects fair value in the broader market. But the actual CLOB order book has no resting sell orders near that price. The strategy places limit orders at Gamma's suggested price, but nobody is willing to sell at those levels on the CLOB.

Why does this happen?

  • Thin markets: UpDown tokens trade in low volume. There are few market makers and limited resting liquidity.
  • Price discovery mismatch: Gamma is aggregating signals from broader markets (crypto exchanges, futures, etc.). The CLOB is isolated and often illiquid at Gamma prices.
  • Our order size: Even small orders may exceed typical CLOB size at that price level.

Why Paper Mode Looked Good

The paper (backtest) system assumed fills at model prices. It simulated instant fills at Gamma's bestAsk without checking actual CLOB liquidity. Paper never interacted with the real order book. That's why it showed 92% win rate on paper—the fills were never questioned.

  • Simulated instant fills
  • No order book interaction

Live Mode (Multiple Losses)

The Core Problem

Paper backtesting made two fatal assumptions: (1) tokens always trade near 50¢, and (2) we can always buy/sell at Gamma's bestAsk. Neither is true in live mode. The real market has thin CLOB liquidity and tokens at much wider price ranges. Backtests trained the model on synthetic data that never matched reality.

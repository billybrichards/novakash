What Happened: The Continuous Evaluator Failure

Fig 1. The failure was THREE compounding problems: broken weights + broken normalisation + bad pricing. The timing concept itself was sound.
Billy's insight is correct: The continuous evaluator failed because of bad thresholds and broken normalisation, NOT because the idea of monitoring the full window is wrong. The concept — "trade early when signal is strong, get cheaper tokens" — is the best edge available. The morning system only worked at T-60s because VPIN was 0.65-0.93 the WHOLE time (cascade), so waiting didn't matter. In normal conditions, the signal may peak at T-180s and be gone by T-60s.
Root Cause Analysis: 3 Independent Bugs
#BugEffectFix in v2
1 Delta weight 7.0 (should be 3.0 max) A tiny 0.05% delta scored 7.0 out of possible 10 → 70% confidence → DECISIVE tier fires instantly Cap delta weight at 3.0. Equal weighting with VPIN max (3.0). A 0.05% delta should score ~1.5, not 7.0.
2 CoinGlass all zeros → normalisation broken Dynamic normalisation: active_max = 3 + 0 + 0 + 0 = 3. Score of 3.0/3.0 = 100% confidence on delta alone. Floor normalisation at 6.0 minimum (assumes VPIN always contributes). Now with CG Standard, we have real data.
3 No minimum time requirement DECISIVE tier could fire at T-290s (10s into window) — maximum uncertainty, price hasn't settled. No trades before T-180s. Minimum 2 minutes of data before ANY tier can fire.

The Opportunity: Why Earlier Entry = Cheaper Tokens

Fig 2. Tokens near window open are ~47¢ (near coin-flip). By T-60s they're ~55¢ (outcome partially priced in). Earlier entry = better R/R if signal quality is sufficient.
The maths: At 55¢ (T-60s entry), you need 55% WR to break even. At 47¢ (T-180s entry), you need only 47% WR. With 92% actual WR, the difference is $0.08 × stake per trade in pure edge. Over 100 trades at $50 avg stake = $400 extra profit from earlier entry alone.
